The Cause Of Water Pollution

There are two main types of water pollution: nonpoint source pollution and point source pollution. Nonpoint source pollution is the result of runoff, coming from sources such as septic tanks, cars, trucks, boats, farms, livestock ranches, and timber harvest areas. Nonpoint source pollution is the most common type of pollution, as it happens much more often than point source pollution. Point source pollution comes from a single source. For instance, oil and chemical spills are both examples of point source pollution. Point source pollution can have an extremely large effect on the environment. Littering has a huge impact on the ocean, too. Trash discarded on the beach or by other water sources finds its way to the ocean, trapping and poisoning marine wildlife and gathering together into huge patches of garbage floating on the ocean.

    How You Can Help

    You can help conserve our oceans and protect our wildlife! Don’t use plastic as much, especially plastic bags. Plastic is one of the main types of litter found in the ocean. Instead, you can use reusable bags. When you do use plastic, make sure to recycle. Every bit counts! You can participate in ocean or river cleanups to personally help get rid of the trash choking our oceans. Help spread the word about the garbage floating in the ocean, support laws about ocean conservation, and support organizations trying to improve the Earth’s environment. These are all ways you can help save the ocean and the Earth.

    Why Should You Care?

    Have you ever been to the beach? Have you heard the gentle crash of the waves and felt the soft sand under your feet? Have you seen The lovely sunsets and the turquoise waters? Because of pollution, these sights and sensations might disappear. Trash and pollution will ruin the beautiful beaches, turning them into landfills and deserts. But you can help stop this.

    As you may know, the Water Cycle is essential to the well-being of the world's ecosystem, and the ocean is the majority of the world's water. If we were to continue to pollute the ocean at the rate we are now, it would destroy the marine ecosystemin its entirety, and that would mean no marine life at all. The collaspe of the Water Cycle would also contribute to toxic rain levels (acidic rain), contanimated drinking water that could potentially kill animals as well as humans, and the depletion of the trees that give us oxygen to breathe.
